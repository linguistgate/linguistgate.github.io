About

I am an educator and writer interested in digital humanities, linguistics, and farming. I like maps and long hikes. One of my dreams is to build a small farmstead with beekeeping, various fermentation capabilities (kimchi, cheese, and Czech pilseners), and an antilibrary.

I teach British and American literature at The Waterford School in Sandy, Utah.

My educational background is in linguistics (Ph.D. and M.A., Stanford University) and German (M.A., Georgetown University). I graduated summa cum laude from the University of California, Irvine, and studied German language and literature at Göttingen.

This website is maintained with the open source Hugo engine and is hosted (currently) on GitHub. The typeface is mostly ET Book because I find it aesthetically pleasing, and I like some of Edward Tufte's ideas. I believe in maintaining control over my content even if the personal webpage has become sadly antiquated, and I hold fast to the idea that the Web should be a space of contributory individual expression, not compartmentalization, reactionary amplification, lock-down, and lock-in. To that extent, my Web presence through Phantisocracy is political.

The title of my public Web presence, Phantisocracy, refers to the utopian ideal set forth by Samuel Taylor Coleridge and Robert Southey in the 1790s. Their pantisocratic ideals were never concretely realized. A phantisocracy is my term of art for any utopian scheme that remains hypothetical.
